Jay Gottschalk

PhD, MS, APRN, AGCNS-BC, CHPN, PMHNP-BC

Education, Certification, and Experience:

Jay has a BA in accounting from MSU Broad College of Business in 2004, an MS in professional accounting from MSU Broad College of Business in 2004, a BSN from University of Illinois at Chicago in 2015, and an MSN from MSU College of Nursing in 2017 in adult-gerontology clinical nurse specialist and nurse education studies. He also has a Post-Master's certificate in psychiatric-mental health nurse practitioner studies from Alverno College in 2021, and a PhD in nursing from Marquette University College of Nursing in 2022. Jay was certified as hospice and palliative nurse (CHPN) in 2013, certified as an adult-gerontology clinical nurse specialist (AGCNS) in 2017, and certified as a psychiatric-mental health nurse practitioner (PMHNP) in 2022.

As a staff nurse, Jay practiced in oncology and palliative care. As a clinical nurse specialist, he practiced in oncology, palliative care, and pain management. As a psychiatric-mental health nurse practitioner, Jay has worked with oncology, palliative care, and general practice. Jay has been practicing as a nurse since 2012 and worked in the Chicagoland, Greater Lansing, Metro Detroit, and Milwaukee areas.

Career Highlights:

“I have had many opportunities throughout my nursing career to help ease the patient journey, either by reducing symptoms related to their disease or treatment, or by helping to reduce the logistical burden of navigating health care. Too, I have been fortunate to be able to help patients, families, and clinicians work on their resilience and perseverance in many settings. More recently, I have been able to help integrate behavioral health into primary care spaces, helping to reduce the time it takes patients to receive specialized care.”

– Jay Gottschalk
